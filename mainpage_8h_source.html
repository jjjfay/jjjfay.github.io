11/*
12 * @mainpage Prosthetic Hand Control through Myoelectric and Pressure Sensors
13 *
14 * @authors Julia Fay & Jack Foxcroft

16 * @section Project Description
17 *
18 * For our project, we will be controlling a robotic gripping device with a pressure sensor
19 * and myoelectric sensors. The robotic hand will be able to rotate about its central axis,
20 * controlled by one myoelectric sensor, and open and close, controlled by the second myoelectric
21 * sensor. The pressure sensor will be attached to the thumb of the gripping device and will make
22 * sure the grip force stays within a desired threshold. This project is centered around a prosthetic
23 * hand device donated to the Empower club. This device was created by a biomedical company called
24 * Ottobock and features a three fingered mechanical hand controlled by a DC motor with an encoder.
25 *
26 * The myoelectric sensors was purchased from sparkfun. The MyoWare 2.0 Muscle Sensor was selected
27 * because it was created for the specific purpose of reading electric signals from muscle movements.
28 * Thus, it features several output types for easy interpretation of the already filtered signal.
29 * Also, this sensor requires a minimum supply of 3.3 volts, which can be easily supplied by the
30 * microcontroller.
31 *
32 * The pressure sensor we selected is a simple and cost-effective part from amazon. It is a thin film
33 * sensor that will easily stick onto the thumb of our gripping device. The sensor only has two wire
34 * connections. One wire is the input, which will be supplied and set to 1V with the use of a resistor.
35 * The second wire is the output, which will be an input to one of the ADCâ€™s on the microcontroller.
36 *
37 * The DC motor we selected is also a simple and cost-effective part. It is a 12V DC motor with an encoder
38 * with a top speed of 100 RPM. A convenient feature of this motor is the shaft output is perpendicular to
39 * the body of the motor allowing for easy attachment to our design. The motor requires a 3.3-5V power supply.
40 *
41 * Overall, our design meets all the rules and requirements of the project. We have two actuators, and two
42 * unique sensors in addition to the encoders on each motor. All our hardware was purchased, or 3D printed.
43 * Our design is safe for users and bystanders as it will be a mainly stationary device that rotates and
44 * moves within a confined area as it will be attached to a steady base. We used the remote controller as
45 * a emergency shutoff switch for our design.
46 *
47 */
